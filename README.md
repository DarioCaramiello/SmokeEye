## 🔄 Validation Workflow

### Step 1 — Plume Shape & Trajectory Validation

> **Scope:** Validate the morphology and trajectory of the smoke plume generated by the wildfire.

- **Dataset:** Sentinel-5P (S5P) — Aerosol Index (AER AI)
- **Rationale:** The aerosol index is sensitive to elevated absorbing aerosol layers (e.g. smoke), making it ideal for tracing plume shape and transport direction at regional scale.

---

### Step 2 — Quantitative Concentration Estimation

> **Scope:** Estimate the quantitative concentration of aerosol/smoke species over the affected area.

- **Dataset:** CAMS NRT (Copernicus Atmosphere Monitoring Service — Near Real Time)
- **Rationale:** CAMS NRT provides gridded atmospheric composition forecasts and analyses, enabling mass concentration retrieval of smoke-related tracers (e.g. organic matter, black carbon).

---

### Step 3 — Near-Source Zone Analysis

> **Scope:** Characterize the combustion-active zone in the immediate vicinity of the fire source.

- **Dataset:** Sentinel-5P (S5P) — NO₂ (Nitrogen Dioxide)
- **Chemical signature:**

$$\text{NO} \xrightarrow{\text{oxidation}} \text{NO}_2$$

  Nitric oxide (NO), emitted directly during active combustion, rapidly oxidizes to nitrogen dioxide (NO₂).  
  NO₂ therefore acts as a **chemical fingerprint of active burning**, allowing identification and spatial delimitation of the fire front.
